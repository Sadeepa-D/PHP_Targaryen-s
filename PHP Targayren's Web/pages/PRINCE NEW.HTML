This is a large painting covering a large area of the wall at the new image house. The two figures are dominating the painting. One is a male figure and the other is a female figure. The female figure is known as Princess Hemamala, a Kalinga princess who brought the sacred Tooth Relic from Kalinga to Sri Lanka in the 4th century CE. The other figure is prince Dantha, her husband who accompanied her. This is a historical event that happened in Sri Lanka and that is recorded in historical texts. The two large figures fill the frame. The couple is shown walking on a forest path. The background seems to be a dense forest. The use of colour by Mendis is so brilliant that it has been able to highlight the couple out of the background.
The colours of this painting are unique. The artist has stuck to a monochrome effect mainly sticking to earth colours. The most prominent colour is a dull ochre shade, Naples yellow and umbers, and sienna shades. The green he has used is olive green and light and dark shades of the colour. The Colour contrast is extremely low, almost absent. Instead, he has created a tonal value contrast yet making sure it is not a notable prominent contrast.
The contrast is mild and less notable. It is only to highlight the human figures and make them pop out of the brownish background. In this specific painting, the background is almost unnoticeable as it naturally sets back and is dull in colour. He has detailed the trees and plants, yet it is in a very low tone.
